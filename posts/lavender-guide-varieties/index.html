💜 A Gentle Introduction to Lavender

— In Every Lavender Plant, A Quiet Woman Hides

By Jojo Len

Some mornings, I brew a cup of hot water with dried lavender buds, and as the steam rises, a gentle fragrance drifts softly, settling deep into the quietest part of my heart.

Lavender is never just a plant to me. It is a rhythm, a hue of emotion, a choice in life—not demanding you to “be better,” but inviting you to “slow down.”

🌿 Why Knowing Lavender Varieties Matters

In the market, they all might be called “Lavender.”
But once planted, you’ll discover some emit a soft, bedtime-story fragrance, while others carry a herbal note reminiscent of old kitchen memories.

Each lavender variety has its own unique scent, appearance, growing habit, and purpose—just like every woman has her own grace and strength.

🪻 English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

“She is a quiet writer with a strong voice”

  • Scent: The purest, most delicate, classic lavender aroma
  • Habit: Hardy and cold-tolerant
  • Uses: Aromatherapy, essential oils, teas, sachets, DIY skincare
  • Best for: Those who love healing scents and making homemade lavender crafts

🫖 Recommended cultivars:

  • ‘Munstead’: Compact and perfect for balconies, with soft fragrance
  • ‘Hidcote’: Deep purple flowers, highly ornamental

She belongs in your diary pages and your bath water.

🪴 French Lavender (Lavandula dentata)

“She is a summer afternoon traveler in a white dress”

  • Scent: Light, fresh, with a subtle green note
  • Habit: Prefers warm, sunny climates
  • Uses: Ornamental, garden decoration, ambiance
  • Best for: Balcony gardeners seeking beauty and atmosphere

🎨 Styling tips:

  • Pair with rosemary and thyme for a fragrant trio
  • Ideal for terracotta pots, creating a charming window view

She doesn’t like the cold but knows how to hold sunshine in her scent.

🦋 Spanish Lavender (Lavandula stoechas)

“She is an outgoing artist with sparkling eyes”

  • Flowers: Butterfly-like bracts on top, visually striking
  • Scent: Strong, herbal with a wild character
  • Habit: Heat-tolerant but frost-sensitive, great for warm climates or containers
  • Best for: Those who love unique shapes, photography, and bold garden statements

📸 Popular cultivars:

  • ‘Anouk’: Classic butterfly flowers, vivid colors
  • ‘Ballerina’: Soft purple bracts like a dancing skirt

She doesn’t need to be quiet—she blooms to express herself.

🌾 Hybrid Lavender (Lavandula × intermedia / Lavandin)

“She is the practical dreamer balancing beauty and efficiency”

  • Scent: Stronger, with a camphor-like note
  • Yield: High, ideal for large plantings
  • Habit: Heat and drought-tolerant, pest resistant
  • Uses: Sachets, dried bouquets, commercial essential oils

🧺 Recommended cultivars:

  • ‘Grosso’: High yield, common in commercial fields
  • ‘Phenomenal’: Ornamental and suitable for larger pots

She is the humble field, quietly abundant.

🌱 Jojo’s Lavender Care Tips

🪴 Balcony growers, here’s how to thrive:

  • Use terracotta or ceramic pots for better airflow
  • Ensure 6+ hours of sunlight daily for blooms
  • Water only when soil is dry to avoid root rot

🪻 Winter care:

  • English lavender can stay outdoors in mild climates
  • Move French and Spanish lavender indoors during cold months
  • Protect roots with straw or burlap if planted in soil

🌸 Keep your lavender fragrant longer:

  • Prune annually in spring to prevent woody stems
  • Harvest flowers when 70% open, then dry for lasting scent
  • Mix with rosemary or sage to create complex aromas

💌 Final Whisper

Lavender doesn’t rush to grow.
She stretches in the sunshine, scents the breeze, and quietly keeps you company when you’re weary.

She exists not to be “useful,” but to be a presence—a gentle companion.
