A Gentle Guide to Verbena Varieties

Monday, Jun 16, 2025 | 6 minute read | Updated at Monday, Jun 16, 2025

@ Jojo Len

Some flowers arrive like a love letter.
Others, like a soft rebellion.

🌿 Verbena, to me, is both—a bloom that doesn’t ask for permission, but chooses the cracks and the edges, flourishing on its own terms.

It’s the flower I turn to when I crave both color and calm. It doesn’t beg for praise or admiration—it simply is, humming in quiet companionship throughout the long days of summer. In my garden, Verbena is less of a guest and more of a guide. She teaches me how to bloom without noise, how to return after drought, and how to stand firm in softness.

Whether you’re looking to brighten a sun-drenched corner or invite butterflies to your balcony, these Verbena varieties are living metaphors of resilience, joy, and feminine strength.

💫 1. Verbena bonariensis – The Tall Dreamer

With slender stems like poetry reaching skyward, Verbena bonariensis sways above the border with a dancer’s grace. Her lavender clusters hover like lavender-colored constellations, attracting bees, butterflies, and dreamers alike.

She reminds me that we’re allowed to take up space with gentleness—that height doesn’t need to be loud to be seen.

🌿 Perfect for: naturalistic gardens, pollinator havens, borders
☀️ Sun: Full sun
📏 Height: Up to 5 ft

🌱 Care tip: Stake tall stems early to prevent flopping, and deadhead regularly to encourage more blooms throughout summer.

💜 2. Verbena rigida – The Grounded Visionary

Low and luminous, Verbena rigida thrives close to the earth with vivid violet blooms and sturdy energy. She’s the steady pulse of a cottage garden, the undercurrent of strength beneath the surface.

She’s the woman who doesn’t chase spotlight but is always at the heart of change.

🌿 Perfect for: ground covers, edges, sunbaked soil
☀️ Sun: Full sun
💧 Water: Drought-tolerant

🌱 Care tip: Allow the soil to dry out between watering; prune lightly after flowering to maintain compact shape.

🌸 3. Homestead Purple – The One That Stays

Among the most beloved, Homestead Purple is all heart. Spilling like velvet across garden beds or over containers, she blooms even in hardship, from early summer till frost. A true constant.

She is the softness that never surrendered—the version of myself I’m always trying to return to.

🌿 Perfect for: trailing, containers, pollinator gardens
💪 Strength: Disease resistant, heat-tolerant

🌱 Care tip: Fertilize monthly with balanced feed during the growing season; pinch back tips early to encourage bushiness.

💗 4. Lanai Series – The Joy Painter

The Lanai Series is the artist in the family. Compact, early blooming, and vibrant in every hue—raspberry pinks, snowy whites, blazing reds—she spreads joy wherever she goes.

A daily reminder that color is not indulgence—it’s a declaration of life.

🌿 Perfect for: hanging baskets, patios, windowsills
🎨 Color palette: wide and vivid

🌱 Care tip: Provide good air circulation in containers to avoid powdery mildew; deadhead faded flowers to prolong bloom time.

🩷 5. Tapien Series – The Quiet Healer

With her fine texture and soothing tones, Tapien varieties blanket the ground like a soft song. Their calming presence soothes neglected corners, thriving where others fade.

Like healing, she’s gentle but expansive—creeping slowly into spaces that needed love.

🌿 Perfect for: low ground cover, containers, calming landscapes
🌈 Shades: lavender, sky blue, soft pinks

🌱 Care tip: Water moderately and mulch to retain moisture; trim back after flowering to keep tidy and encourage new growth.

🔥 6. Superbena Series – The Bold Survivor

From the trusted Proven Winners line, the Superbena series brings flower power with substance. Heat? She thrives. Pests? She shrugs. She is beautiful, bold, and completely unbothered.

She is every woman who stopped asking for permission and started planting her own future.

🌿 Perfect for: high-performance gardens, balconies, beginner gardeners
🛡 Traits: mildew resistant, vigorous bloomers

🌱 Care tip: Plant in well-drained soil; avoid overhead watering to reduce mildew risk; deadhead occasionally for continuous blooms.

🎨 7. Verbena hybrida – The Garden Muse

The hybrids are many—and marvelous. Verbena hybrida mixes color and form with joyful abandon. Whether you lean classic or quirky, there’s one that feels like you.

Plant them like paint strokes. Let them reflect your mood and your moment.

🌿 Perfect for: colorful borders, mood gardens
💐 Colors: from jewel tones to soft blush

🌱 Care tip: Feed monthly with liquid fertilizer; keep soil moist but not soggy; deadhead to maintain vibrant flowering.

✨ 8. Quartz Series – The Steady Flame

Strong and structured, the Quartz Series stands firm through sun and storm. Their upright growth and dependable blooming make them perfect companions for those seeking simplicity with soul.

Because sometimes, what you need most is a quiet kind of reliability.

🌿 Perfect for: mass plantings, windy spots
🧘 Vibe: stable, enduring, peaceful

🌱 Care tip: Water regularly during dry spells; remove spent blooms to encourage prolonged flowering; can be cut back mid-season for renewed growth.

🌼 Growing Gentle Power: Why I Keep Coming Back to Verbena

In a garden full of flowers that shout, Verbena is the one who hums. She thrives in sun, sings with butterflies, and reminds us that blooming doesn’t have to be dramatic to be real.
Let her fill your containers, cascade down your walls, stretch across your beds—and settle softly in your heart.

💡 FAQ: Gentle Questions About Growing Verbena

Q: What is the easiest Verbena to grow for beginners?
A: Homestead Purple and the Superbena Series are among the easiest. They are low-maintenance, disease-resistant, and flower generously.

Q: Does Verbena attract butterflies and bees?
A: Yes! Most Verbena varieties are excellent for pollinator gardens. Especially Verbena bonariensis and Lanai types.

Q: Can Verbena grow in containers or hanging baskets?
A: Absolutely. Trailing varieties like Lanai, Tapien, and Superbena do beautifully in pots and hanging containers.

Q: How much sun does Verbena need?
A: Full sun (6–8 hours per day) is ideal for vigorous growth and vibrant blooms.

Q: Is Verbena an annual or perennial?
A: It depends on your zone. In colder climates, it’s grown as an annual. In USDA Zones 7–11, some types can be perennial.
