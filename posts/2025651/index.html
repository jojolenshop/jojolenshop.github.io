Comprehensive Guide to Rose Varieties | Beginner's Gardening Tips by Jojo Len

Thursday, Jun 5, 2025 | 3 minute read | Updated at Thursday, Jun 5, 2025

@ Jojo Len

Roses—often called the queen of flowers—are not only a symbol of romance, but also a favorite of gardeners around the world. For those who are just beginning their gardening journey, understanding the different types of roses and their basic care is the very first step toward creating a slow-living garden of your own.

Today, I want to share with you, in the gentlest way possible, eight classic rose varieties and practical growing tips—so you can enjoy the beauty of blooms right at home.

⸻

Eight Classic Rose Varieties

  1. Hybrid Tea Rose Known as the most iconic rose for cutting, hybrid teas bear large, elegant single blooms in a wide range of colors and enjoy a long flowering season. They are perfect for floral arrangements and brightening up your indoor space.

Care tips: Needs full sun (at least 6 hours daily) and well-drained soil. Avoid overwatering and keep the leaves dry to prevent fungal diseases. Regular pruning of deadwood encourages new growth.

⸻

  1. Grandiflora Rose

A cross between hybrid teas and floribundas, grandifloras feature clusters of large blooms with extended blooming periods. Ideal for gardens and container pots alike.

Care tips: Feed with rich compost, ensure good air circulation to avoid blackspot and powdery mildew—especially during damp weather.

⸻

  1. Shrub Rose These bushy, vigorous growers are known for their disease resistance and dense foliage. They bloom in various forms and are ideal for flower beds or borders.

Care tips: Great for beginners. Just provide moderate watering and occasional pruning.

⸻

  1. David Austin English Rose These English roses combine the strong fragrance of old roses with the form and repeat bloom of modern ones. They are beloved for their romantic style.

Care tips: Thrive in sunny, well-ventilated areas. Keep the soil moist but not soggy. Feed regularly to boost flowering.

⸻

  1. Old Garden Rose Steeped in history and rich in scent, these heirloom varieties are often shrub-like and well-suited to traditional gardens.

⸻

  1. Climbing Rose With long, vigorous canes, climbing roses are perfect for trellises, arches, or fences. They bloom singly or in clusters over a long season.

Care tips: Train and tie the canes to encourage direction, maintain airflow, and stay alert for pests.

⸻

  1. Miniature Rose Compact and charming, miniature roses are excellent for pots, balconies, or even indoor spaces.

⸻

  1. Groundcover Rose Low-growing and sprawling, groundcover roses offer abundant blooms with minimal care. They’re hardy and disease-tolerant.

⸻

🌱 Beginner-Friendly Rose Care Tips

No matter which variety you fall in love with, these essentials will help your roses thrive: • Sunlight is key: Roses need at least 6 hours of sun daily to flourish. • Water wisely: Keep the soil moist but not soggy; avoid getting the leaves wet. • Well-draining soil: Loamy, fertile soil with compost works best. • Regular pruning: Remove dead or weak branches to improve airflow and encourage new growth. • Pest & disease control: Keep the area clean. Natural sprays like diluted soap water can help manage pests.

⸻

🌸 Tending Roses, Tending Life

Growing roses is more than cultivating plants—it’s a quiet conversation with nature, a way to nurture beauty and breathe softness into our daily rhythms. Watching a bud slowly unfurl, or listening to the wind rustle the leaves—it all becomes a ritual of tenderness.

If you’re new to roses, don’t worry. With this beginner’s guide, everything—from choosing varieties to gentle care—will feel doable and even joyful.
